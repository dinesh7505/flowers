(A)A short note on lotus

1.The lotus flower is regarded as the National Flower of India.
2.Lotus symbolises beauty, purity and knowledge.
3.It is an aquatic flower with round leaves floating on the water.
4.It grows in freshwater lakes and shallow ponds.
5.It is a beautiful flower which has an appealing fragrance and colour.

(B) Why lotus is our national Flower?

1.The lotus was chosen as the national flower because it enjoyed a significant presence in ancient traditions, scriptures and mythology.
2.The Bhagavad Gita considers it a metaphor for detachment: Just as the lotus remains untouched by the muddy waters in which it grows, human beings should rise above worldly attachments.

(C) Why is the lotus flower important?

1.The Lotus flower is regarded in many different cultures, especially in eastern religions, as a symbol of purity, enlightenment, self-regeneration and rebirth.
2.Its characteristics are a perfect analogy for the human condition: even when its roots are in the dirtiest waters, the Lotus produces the most beautiful flower.
